# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

This project is an educational resource for students. We are committed to making participation in this project a respectful and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of academic level, background, or experience with computer architecture.

---

## Our Standards

**Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints. There are often multiple valid approaches to a problem
- Gracefully accepting constructive feedback on your code
- Focusing on what is best for the learning goals of the project
- Helping fellow students understand concepts rather than just handing them answers

**Examples of unacceptable behavior:**

- Submitting work that is not your own without attribution
- Dismissing or mocking contributions from less experienced students
- Harassment, personal attacks, or trolling in issues, pull requests, or comments
- Sharing solutions in a way that undermines the academic integrity of COE 341 assignments
- Any form of plagiarism

---

## Academic Integrity

This repository is intended for **learning and building upon**, not for copying solutions wholesale. When you fork and contribute, you are expected to understand what you write. Submitting code you do not understand as your own assignment work violates your university's academic integrity policy.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a private issue or contacting the project maintainers directly. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.
